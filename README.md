## Day 2 – Global E-commerce Sales & Profit Optimization
Realized that Dashboard Building is not showing multiple visuals at on go, rather it is the way you answer a business problem.
To perform any analysis there should be a proper structure that is 
* Start with a BUSINESS PROBLEM
* Define the GOAL of your DASHBOARD
* Walk through key VISUALS
* Highlight the Key INSIGHTS
* Show Business Impact
* Build a Effective Story

Starting with the first step, I have build an Business Requirement Document(BRD) that states what is the objective and key insights to get from the data
# Global E-commerce Sales & Profit Optimization

## Objective
To analyze global e-commerce sales data and identify actionable insights that improve **revenue, profitability, and operational efficiency** across markets, customers, and products.

---

## Business Goals

- Increase overall **profit margins** across all regions  
- Identify **high-performing and underperforming markets**  
- Optimize **discount and pricing strategies**  
- Reduce **unnecessary operational and shipping costs**  
- Improve **customer profitability and retention**  
- Eliminate or improve **loss-making products**

---

## Key Business Questions / Requirements

### 1. Revenue vs Profit Analysis
- Identify regions where **high sales do not translate into profit**
- Analyze impact of **discounts and shipping costs on profit margins**
- Highlight **loss-making regions and root causes**

---

### 2. Market Performance Evaluation
- Compare **markets and regions based on profitability (not just sales)**
- Identify:
  - High Sales + High Profit → *Scale opportunities*
  - High Sales + Low Profit → *Optimization needed*
  - Low Sales + Low Profit → *Deprioritize*

---

### 3. Discount Effectiveness
- Analyze relationship between **discount and sales volume**
- Determine:
  - Optimal discount levels
  - Scenarios where discounts reduce profitability
- Identify **over-discounted transactions**

---

### 4. Shipping Cost Optimization
- Evaluate shipping cost as a % of sales
- Identify:
  - Regions with high shipping expenses
  - Inefficient shipping modes
  - Impact of order priority on cost

---

### 5. Customer Profitability Analysis
- Identify:
  - Most profitable customers
  - High-revenue but low-profit customers
- Analyze customer dependency:
  - % of revenue from top customers
- Segment customers based on **profit contribution**

---

### 6. Product Performance Analysis
- Identify:
  - Loss-making products
  - High-performing categories and sub-categories
- Evaluate:
  - Profitability by category
  - Products with consistent negative margins
- Recommend:
  - Price changes or discontinuation

---

### 7. Order & Logistics Efficiency
- Analyze alignment between:
  - Order Priority vs Shipping Mode
- Identify:
  - Cost inefficiencies in logistics decisions
- Recommend optimization strategies

---

### 8. Seasonal & Time-Based Trends
- Identify:
  - Monthly/quarterly sales and profit trends
  - Peak demand periods
- Evaluate:
  - Whether business is maximizing high-demand periods

---

### 9. Geographic Profitability
- Analyze:
  - Profitability at city, state, and country level
- Identify:
  - High volume but low-profit locations
- Provide insights on:
  - Pricing, logistics, or operational gaps

---

### 10. Quantity vs Profit Relationship
- Analyze:
  - Whether bulk orders increase profitability
- Identify:
  - Cases where high quantity leads to lower margins

---

### 11. Segment-Wise Analysis
- Compare:
  - Consumer vs Corporate vs Home Office
- Identify:
  - Most profitable segment
- Recommend:
  - Targeting and marketing focus

---

### 12. Pricing Strategy Evaluation
- Assess:
  - Consistency of pricing across regions
- Identify:
  - Regions with aggressive discounting
- Recommend:
  - Region-specific pricing strategies

## Expected Outcome

This analysis will help:
- Make **data-driven decisions**
- Improve **profitability**
- Optimize **pricing and logistics**
- Identify **growth opportunities**
- Reduce **business risks**
